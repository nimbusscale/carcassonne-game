# Carcassonne — Rules

*A tile-laying game for 2 to 5 players by Klaus-Jürgen Wrede. Ages 8 and up.*

---

## Game Components

- **72 Land tiles** (including 1 Start tile with a darker back) showing roads, cities, fields, and monasteries. The 12 River tiles are not part of the basic game but share the same back as the Start tile.
- **40 followers** in 5 colors. Each follower can become a thief, a knight, a monk, or a farmer. Each player uses one follower as a score marker.
- **1 scoreboard** to track players' scores.
- **1 rulebook and 1 player aid**

---

## Game Overview

On their turn, players place one tile, gradually creating roads, cities, monasteries, and fields. To score points, players place followers on these tiles. Points are scored throughout the game and at the end. The player with the most points wins.

---

## Game Setup

1. Place the Start tile face-up in the center of the table.
2. Shuffle the remaining tiles and arrange them in face-down stacks accessible to all players.
3. Place the scoreboard near the edge of the table.
4. Each player takes their 8 followers and places one (the **score marker**) on space **0** of the scoreboard (lower right corner).
5. Each player keeps their remaining **7 followers** in front of them as their personal supply.
6. Choose a first player however you like, or let the youngest player begin.

---

## Playing the Game

Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, you **must** do the following in order:

1. **Draw and place one new Land tile.**
2. **Optionally place one follower** from your supply onto the tile you just placed.
3. **Score any completed roads, cities, and/or monasteries** immediately.

Your turn is then over and play passes clockwise.

---

## 1. Placing Land Tiles

Draw one face-down Land tile, show it to all players, then place it according to these rules:

- The new tile must touch **at least one side** of a previously placed tile. Corner-only connections are not allowed.
- New tiles must always **continue** existing fields, cities, and/or roads.

> **Unplaceable tile:** In the unlikely event that a drawn tile cannot legally be placed (all players must agree), remove it from the game and draw another tile.

---

## 2. Placing Followers

After placing a tile, you may place **one follower** from your supply onto that tile. Rules:

- You may only place **1 follower per turn**.
- The follower must come from your **personal supply**.
- The follower must be placed on the **tile you just placed**.
- You may **not** place a follower on a road, city, or field section that is already connected to another tile containing any follower (regardless of color).

### Follower Roles

| Role | Placed on |
|------|-----------|
| **Thief** | A road section |
| **Knight** | A city section |
| **Monk** | A monastery |
| **Farmer** | A field section (lay it flat) |

If you have placed all your followers, you simply place a tile each turn. You may **never** voluntarily remove a follower from a tile. Followers are only returned to your supply when the feature they occupy is scored.

---

## 3. Scoring Completed Features

Whenever a tile placement completes one or more features, those features are scored **immediately**, before the next player's turn.

### Completed Road

A road is complete when both ends connect to a crossroad, a city section, a monastery, or loop back on itself.

- The player with a **thief** on a completed road scores **1 point per tile** in the road.
- Count tiles, not sections (a single tile can contain more than one road section).

### Completed City

A city is complete when it is fully enclosed by walls with no gaps.

- The player with a **knight** in a completed city scores **2 points per tile** in the city.
- Each **banner** in the city is worth an additional **2 points**.
- When counting tiles: a tile with two city sections still counts as only **1 tile**.

### Completed Monastery

A monastery is complete when it is surrounded on all 8 sides by tiles.

- The player with a **monk** in a completed monastery scores **9 points** (1 point per surrounding tile, including the monastery tile itself).

### Ties: Multiple Followers on the Same Feature

Clever tile placement can merge road or city sections, resulting in multiple thieves or knights on the same feature. The player with the **most** followers scores all the points. In case of a **tie**, all tied players score the full points.

### Returning Followers After Scoring

After a road, city, or monastery is scored, all followers on that feature are returned to their owners' supplies. It is possible to place a follower, complete a feature with that same tile, score it, and have the follower returned to your supply — all in one turn. The order is always:

1. Complete a feature with the new tile.
2. Place a thief, knight, or monk (optional).
3. Score the completed feature.
4. Return the followers to their supplies.

---

## Fields and Farmers

Connected field sections form larger fields. Fields are **not scored during the game.**

- Farmers are placed **lying flat** to indicate they stay in place.
- Farmers are **never returned** to a player's supply until final scoring.
- Fields are separated from one another by roads, cities, and the edge of the board.

---

## End of Game

The game ends after the player who places the **last tile** finishes their turn. Final scoring then takes place.

---

## Final Scoring

### Incomplete Roads, Cities, and Monasteries

- **Roads and monasteries:** 1 point per tile.
- **Cities:** 1 point per tile + 1 point per banner.
- Tie rules are the same as for completed features.
- After scoring, return all followers to their supplies.

### Scoring Farmers

Farmers are only scored at the end of the game. Rules:

- Only **completed cities** count for farmer scoring.
- A farmer scores for any completed city that its field **touches** (distance does not matter).
- The player with the **most farmers** in a given field scores **3 points per completed city** that field touches. The size of the city does not matter.
- In case of a tie, all tied players score the full points.
- A field only scores points if it touches at least one completed city.
- More than one field may touch — and score for — the same city.

Once all fields are scored, the game is over. **The player with the most points wins.** In case of a tie, play another game to determine the winner.

---

## Scoring Summary

### During the Game (Completed Features)

| Feature | Scorer | Points |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Road | Thief | 1 point per tile |
| City | Knight | 2 points per tile + 2 points per banner |
| Monastery | Monk | 9 points total |

### At the End of the Game (Incomplete Features)

| Feature | Scorer | Points |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Road | Thief | 1 point per tile |
| City | Knight | 1 point per tile + 1 point per banner |
| Monastery | Monk | 1 point per tile (monastery + each surrounding tile) |

### Farmers (End of Game Only)

| Feature | Scorer | Points |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Completed city adjacent to field | Farmer (majority) | 3 points per completed city |

> **Scoring track note:** If your marker passes space 50, lay it on its side to indicate 50+ points and continue moving normally.
